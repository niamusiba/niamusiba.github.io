I hope that by the end of this class, HTML and CSS and coding as a whole concept don’t seem like such an enormous terrifying concept to swallow, but perhaps instead some slightly too big chunks of concepts that might still be scary but not as impossible to handle. I wouldn’t quite call them butterflies, but it’s a nice and fairly easy to navigate site. What gets me more than anything are their beautiful products and product photography. Week Two

Prompt: What are you planning to do for project 1? What content do you plan to show? How will you make each page unique while maintaining a cohesive design across the project? How will your color palette, imagery, and typography choice complement your subject matter?

For project one I am making a site inspired by the girl groups of the early 2000s and late 90s. I also am very inspired by clothing catalogs, teen magazines, and the general aesthetic of that time. I thought it might be fun to make up a fake girl group name for the house that I live in, and design a site around my three roommates and myself as if we were the members of this imaginary group. This week we will all style ourselves as our personas and I will art direct a shoot so that I have content for the photos that will be on the site. On top of those photos, I want to have fun stickers and maybe even handwritten elements to make it really feel like the time period. The content for copy will be very silly and surface level snippets about each member of the house.

I think I’ll be able to really tie together a visual style with a color palette and with the style and treatment of the photos. For the sake of time I will be using a digital camera but I want to buck up and use Photoshop so that I can make the images look textured like film photo or scanned photos, and I will have images cut out to help add to the general aesthetic of the site. For type I need to do some more research on typefaces of the early 2000s but I already have a general feel in mind for some fun bubbly decorative typefaces paired with pretty basic body type.

Here’s my Pinterest board for the project!

Week Eight

Prompt: Read this article on mobile-first design and share your thoughts on mobile-first vs desktop first.

I think that mobile first makes so much sense. In this digitally evolving world, the natural instinct should be to design for the least advanced interface first (mobile) and then work your way up. I can't count how many times I've lost interest in a brand/company/whatever because they have a horribe website, and most of the time my first impression of a website is when I'm viewing it from my phone. I think when you have a really nice mobile website it makes a huge difference. Not only this, but designing for mobile first is also designing to be more accessible, considering mobile phones have more sales than desktop at this point. All in all it just makes sense to me that mobile first would be the way to design!

Week Nine

Prompt: This one's about "why." Read this article (try to pretend it's not a cheezy article about business success). Spend some time thinking about why you are pursuing a career in design. Really dig down, do the 7 "whys" tool in the article. OR If that's too challenging right now, consider people in your life or people who've inspired you. Think about what drives them, what their "why" is. Why did they work so hard to do XYZ, was it from past experiences they had, was it because they saw a need they were called to fill? What's their deeper "why?" Ask them if you know them, or look up some biographical information and try to figure it out. Write about either you or your inspiration

I've been questioning my "why" for this whole term. When I entered design, my "why" was that I was too scared to be an art practice major (or that's what I thought it was) and design seemed like a cool way to do art but work around my fears and insecurities surrounding actually labling myself as an artist. Now, two and a half years later, my "why" has changed a lot. At the end of the day, I am becoming a designer because it is a tool, and a powerful one that can enact a lot of change. I'm doing it to learn new skills, meet new creatives, and open doors for myself. I am doing it because there needs to be more diversity in creative fields and design is a platform that I can use to uplift other Black and brown creatives. I'm doing it because it helps me communicate and understand things better for myself and for others. And I'm doing it because I wish there had been a badass Black woman designer to look up to when I was in high school trying to figure out how I could turn my creative energy into an actual job or life, and so I want to be that person.
